Vanishing Populations

This visual story portrays the fragility of Whooping Crane populations in Wisconsin due to increasing habitat loss. Many conservation organizations do incredible work, and it is our job to donate to these groups and vote for representatives who will act alongside them to protect wildlife habitats. I was inspired to make this story after visiting the International Crane Foundation this past summer. The crane exhibits, historical lessons, and restored prairies make for a truly fascinating and enjoyable experience. This story should speak to caring individuals who want to protect natural beauty, including habitats and wildlife. Even if previously uninterested in cranes, I hope that this encourages interest and action towards crane conservation. To analyze spatial patterns, I used Wisconsin county boundaries data from the DNR, protected wetlands and marshes data from the USFWS, and I manually transferred data from the International Crane Foundation’s “Where are the Whoopers?” map.
